Stephen Curry

Wardell Stephen Curry II was born on March 14, 1988. He is an American professional basketball player for the Golden State Warriors of the National Basketball Association (NBA) and wears #30. He is 6 feet and 3 inches tall and weighs 190 pounds.He went to Davidson from 2006-2009 for college.

Steph is raised by Sonya and Dell Curry. Dell was a former NBA player for the Utah Jazz, Cleveland Cavaliers, Charlotte Hornets, Milwaukee Bucks, and Toronto Raptors. Sonya played volleyball at Virginia Tech and is currently a school administrator. Steph has a younger brother named Seth. Seth is also a current NBA player, currently playing for the Portland Trail Blazers.

After being drafted by the Warriors in 2009, Curry did sensational in his rookie year, finishing the year second in rookie of the year voting. However, injuries, especially foot and ankle injuries plagued his next couple years, as he missed several games due to them. However, in 2013, things changed. His injury problem got better, and he played more games that year. He finished with a career high of 24 points a game and also broke the single season three point record that year with 272 threes.

After hiring new head coach Steve Kerr for the 2015 season, Curry had his best season by far. His new system gave Curry more freedom to shoot, helping the team evolve into a title contender. Curry won his first ever MVP , leading the Warriors to a championship that year, finally bringing one to the bay after a 40 year drought.

After failing to become back to back champions in 2016, Curry and the Warriors regrouped, adding some key players to redempt themselves in 2017, winning their second title in three years. In 2018, the Warriors reached the Finals once again, this time sweeping the Cavs 4-0 to become back to back champions. This year, in 2019, the Warriors start their quest for a three-peat.

Curry plays almost exclusively at the point guard position and has career averages of 23.1 points, 6.8 assists, 4.4 rebounds, and 1.8 steals per game. He has made four All-NBA teams and has won two MVPs in his career so far. He is considered to be an important leader within the Warriors organization; he is known for his humility and work ethic. Curry currently ranks fourth in NBA history in career three-point field goal percentage and also holds four of the top five seasons in terms of total three-pointers made. He is also the fastest player in league history to make 2,000 career three-pointers, doing so in 227 fewer games than the previous record-holder, Ray Allen. Curry is also a sensational free throw shooter, with a carrer free throw percentage of just over 90%. He is currently the Warriors' all-time free throw leader, and has also led the NBA in free throw percentage in four seasons. Curry is currently married longtime girlfriend and Toronto native Ayesha Alexander. Together, they have two daughters and a son, Riley (born 2012), Ryan (born 2015), and Canon (born 2018). They currently live in Alamo, California.
